# AI Trends Q1 2026 — Summary Report

**Sources**: Microsoft, IBM, MIT Sloan Management Review, MIT Technology Review, TechCrunch, PwC, VentureBeat, Google Cloud, Barclays Research

## Top AI Trends for Q1 2026

### 1. Agentic AI Goes Mainstream
AI agents are no longer experimental — they are being built directly into enterprise platforms. Multi-agent systems (MAS) are enabling higher-value, function-specific automation across business operations. However, Gartner places GenAI in the "trough of disillusionment," and analysts predict agentic AI may follow.

### 2. From Hype to Pragmatism — "Show Me the Money"
2026 is the year enterprises demand real ROI from AI investments. The focus is shifting away from building ever-larger models toward making AI usable and demonstrating measurable productivity gains. PwC expects more companies to adopt enterprise-wide AI strategies with top-down leadership.

### 3. Rise of Efficient and On-Device Models
Alongside massive frontier models, 2026 sees the emergence of smaller, hardware-aware models running on modest accelerators. On-device AI and edge intelligence are reaching mainstream adoption, enabling deployment where large cloud models are impractical.

### 4. Chinese Open-Source Models Gaining Ground
Chinese AI firms are rapidly closing the gap with Western frontier labs. Expect more Silicon Valley applications to quietly ship on top of Chinese open-source models, with the lag shrinking from months to weeks.

### 5. AI for Scientific Discovery
AI is moving beyond summarization to actively participating in scientific research — generating hypotheses and collaborating with human researchers in physics, chemistry, and biology.

### 6. Enterprise AI Shifts from Individual to Platform
AI is evolving from isolated individual tools to enterprise-wide platforms that orchestrate workflows, connect data across departments, and coordinate team activities. This represents a management revolution, not just a technology upgrade.

### 7. AI Regulation Intensifies
Political battles over AI governance are escalating, with federal and state authorities competing for regulatory authority while AI companies lobby aggressively. No resolution is in sight.

### 8. Security for AI Agents
As organizations deploy more autonomous agents, building trust through security becomes essential. Agents need security protections comparable to human employees.

### 9. World Models as the Next Frontier
Researchers see world models — AI systems that learn how things move and interact in 3D spaces — as the next major leap beyond LLMs.

### 10. AIaaS Market Booming
The global AI-as-a-Service market is projected to grow from $17.14B (2025) to $123.89B by 2031, a CAGR of 39%.
